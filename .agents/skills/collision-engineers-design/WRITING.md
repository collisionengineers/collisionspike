# Collision Engineers — Writing Reference

The single source of truth for **how Collision Engineers writes** — across emails, expert reports,
valuations, rebuttals, fee-note covering notes and status chasers. Distilled from the firm's
historical sent correspondence, worked-example documents, and the authoritative `ce-house-style`
skill. Pair this with the visual rules in `README.md` (§3 Visual Foundations, Documents & reports)
and the document templates in `ui_kits/documents/`.

> **One sentence:** *Communicate as an independent vehicle engineering expert — concise, professional,
> evidence-based, calm under challenge, and confident without being confrontational.*

---

## 1. Voice & positioning

Calm, professional, technically authoritative. Every message positions CE as an **independent
expert**: fact-driven not emotive, confident but measured, cooperative even in disagreement. No sales
tone, no exaggeration, no politeness padding. **Authority comes from clarity and justification, not
forceful language.**

## 2. Tone

- **Primary:** professional, neutral, courteous, assured.
- **Contextual modifiers:** *supportive* (helping handlers/clients), *firm* (defending reports,
  valuations, audit positions), *concise* (routine delivery), *detailed* (disputes & technical
  challenges).
- Emotive language is avoided. Frustration, urgency or disagreement is expressed **factually, never
  emotionally** — words like "frustrating" or "unacceptable" do not appear.

## 3. Mechanics

- **British English** throughout: *-ise* spellings (*authorise, prioritise, recognise*), *colour*,
  *roadworthy*. Currency as `£1,200.00`. Dates as `dd/mm/yyyy` or `5th May 2026`.
- **Casing:** Title Case for document titles & service names; Sentence case for body; **UPPERCASE**
  for document section headings and eyebrows.
- **Person:** "We" for the firm, "you" for the reader/client; reports speak in measured third person.
- **No emoji, no slang, no exclamation marks.**

## 4. Structure

**Greeting** — simple and direct: *"Good morning,"*, *"Good afternoon,"*, *"Hi [Name],"*. Formal
documents open *"Dear Sirs"* / *"FAO The Court"* / *"FAO: The Instructing Solicitor"*.

**Body** — one of three shapes:
- *Report / document delivery* (most common, very short): *"Please see attached [report / estimate /
  audit]. Any issues let us know. Kind regards, [Name]"*.
- *Technical clarification / defence*: a clear opening statement, then logical sequencing — **what was
  done, why it was done, why it is reasonable / justified** — and a calm closing reassurance.
- *Chasing / status*: polite but direct; no apology unless responsibility is owned; a clear request
  for action.

**Conciseness spectrum** — default to brevity; expand only where justification is required.

| Situation | Length |
| --- | --- |
| Report delivery | Very short |
| Clarification | Short–medium |
| Dispute / defence | Medium–long |
| Internal coordination | Short |

## 5. Preferred wording

- *"Please see attached…"* · *"Any issues let us know."*
- *"We have reviewed…"* · *"We consider this reasonable because…"*
- *"We have followed [guideline / rate / standard]…"*
- *"We consider"*, *"the evidence indicates"*, *"on the information provided"* — where a judgement
  needs a boundary.

Statements are assertive but **evidence-based, citing the source** where it helps: ABP rates, Audatex,
Glass's Guide, Cazana, CAP HPI, Thatcham, FOS guidance, ABI guidance, market data, safety procedures.

**Report register** (more formal): *"In accordance with your instructions received on … our findings
are as detailed below:"* · *"We consider that an equitable settlement would be …"* · *"It is our
professional opinion that …"* · *"We have proceeded on that basis throughout."* · *"This addendum is
to be read alongside our original report and supersedes nothing contained within it."*

## 6. Independence framing — the most important line

Reinforce, where relevant, that CE has no financial interest in the claim outcome, acts as an
independent assessor, and bases conclusions on industry standards and evidence. The standard line —
use it to close most disputes, because it reframes the matter as **independent judgement, not
negotiation**:

> *"We confirm that we have no financial interest in the settlement of this claim and see no reason to
> deviate from our independent report."*

## 7. Conflict & dispute handling

When challenged by a TPI (third-party insurer) or other party: do not escalate emotionally; respond
with technical rationale; frame conclusions as **professional judgements, not opinions**. Useful
framings: *"We see no reason to deviate…"*, *"This is consistent with…"*, *"The claimant is entitled
to…"*. Firm disagreement, expressed without antagonism.

### Canonical query responses
Fill the `[bracketed]` figures from the actual case, keep the independence line, add only what the
specific query needs. Use as the backbone of a reply — not a script to paste blind.

**TPI wants a total loss; the vehicle is repairable**
> The Defendant insurers are liable up to the cost of replacement of the Claimant's vehicle. In this
> instance, the repair costs are only **[X]%** of the vehicle value, so this claim is a repairable
> proposition. In addition, we have agreed the repairs on a contract basis and noted the report
> accordingly — costs are fixed, and the Defendant insurer is safe in the knowledge that the repair
> costs cannot increase. The Claimant's vehicle has been damaged through the negligent driving of the
> Defendant insurer's policyholder. It would be wholly unreasonable for the Defendant insurer to force
> the Claimant into a total loss settlement to fit their financial interests. We confirm that we have
> no financial interest in the settlement of this claim and see no reason to deviate from our
> independent report.

*(The "contract basis / fixed costs" sentence only applies where repairs were actually agreed on that
basis — drop it otherwise.)*

**Retail repair — defending the specification**
> Please advise the TPI that we have produced a detailed repair specification in line with the
> Claimant's entitlement under current law. All our charges are within the 'ABP guide to retail
> charges', and are all required to restore the vehicle to its pre-accident condition. We have not
> been provided with any evidence that would confirm our repair specification is high/excessive. We
> can confirm we have no financial interest in the settlement of this claim, and we see no reason to
> deviate from our repair specification.

**Salvage — standard response**
> On review we would consider the salvage rate within our report as fair and reasonable considering
> the vehicle's value, the repair costs exceeding the vehicle value, and the salvage category applied.
> The TP rate will be based on their increased purchasing power / agreements with salvage agents,
> something the client will not be privy to on the open market. We see no reason to deviate from our
> report.

**Salvage — final response**
> The TPI have no legal entitlement to the salvage of the vehicle, and it would be unreasonable for
> them to impose their salvage value. We have independently valued the salvage and, given the
> structural damage and the repair costs being over **[X]%** of the vehicle value, we believe our
> value to be fair and reasonable. We are only here due to the negligence of the TP policyholder; for
> the TP to now impose their contracted salvage rates would be wholly unreasonable.

## 8. Do

- State **what was reviewed → what was found → the engineering or valuation reason**.
- Separate evidence, judgement, and limitations.
- Justify decisions with a standard or piece of evidence.
- Close politely, without flourish.

## 9. Avoid — this is enforced

The house style explicitly bars two categories from any client/court output:

- **AI tell-tales:** *"it is important to note"*, *"delve"*, *"comprehensive"*, *"seamless"*, caveat
  stacking, and similar filler that reads as generated text.
- **Internal workflow terms:** *EVA* (as an internal system), *"guide uplift"*, *"prompt"*, *"mode"*,
  *"AI"*, *"tool output"*, *"draft strategy"* — anything describing **how** the work is produced.
  *(Note: "EVA" legitimately appears as the name of an opposing assessor — "Exclusive Vehicle
  Assessors" — in rebuttals; that usage is fine.)*
- Also avoid: sales/marketing language; apologies unless responsibility is owned; sounding defensive
  or emotional; criticising the instructed party or opposing expert personally; unsupported absolutes
  where the evidence is incomplete.

## 10. Document types & sign-off

The letterhead template (see `ui_kits/documents/`) carries all of these:

| Document | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Repairable / Contract Repair Report | Independent accident damage report, repairable outcome. |
| Total Loss Report | Damage report where the vehicle is a write-off; settlement = engineer value − salvage. |
| Market Valuation Evidence | Retail pre-accident value, evidenced by live comparable adverts. |
| Addendum Report | Further commentary defending a figure under challenge; read alongside the original. |
| Diminution Rebuttal | Rejecting a third-party diminution-in-value claim on methodology & evidence. |
| Roadworthy / Criminal Reports | Safety/compliance determinations and criminal-matter reports. |
| Part 35 Responses | Written answers to a court's Schedule of Questions to the Engineer. |
| Fee Note | VAT invoice for engineering work. |

- **Reports are frequently multi-page.** The supplied examples run to 3–16 pages; continuation pages
  repeat a one-line running head (Our Ref / Your Ref / Date) and the standard footer.
- **Some documents are signed.** Signature images live in `assets/signatures/`
  (`andy_patterson.png`, `ed_mawdsley.png`, `neil_oreilly.png`) — transparent PNGs to drop above the
  engineer's typed name and *"Independent Automotive Engineer, Collision Engineers Ltd"*.
- **Governance:** valuation, roadworthy, rebuttal, salvage and damage-estimating outputs remain
  AI-assisted **drafts until a named human signs off**. House-style formatting never authorises a send.
