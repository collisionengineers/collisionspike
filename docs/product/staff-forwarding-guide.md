# Forwarding provider emails to the instructions mailbox

During the trial, new work arrives by forwarding provider emails to
**instructions@collisionengineers.co.uk**. Follow these rules so every forwarded case arrives
complete — with its documents, its photos, and the right work provider.

## The rules

1. **Use the normal Forward button.** A plain forward carries the provider's original
   attachments with it, which is exactly what the system needs. Do **not** use
   "Forward as attachment" — an email wrapped up inside another email keeps its documents
   readable but its **photos cannot be brought into the case**.
2. **One provider email per forward.** Forward each instruction or photo email on its own.
   Don't combine several into one message, and don't add other recipients.
3. **Instructions and photo emails only.** The trial covers new instructions and the photo
   emails that belong with them. Queries, chasers and anything else stay in the normal
   mailboxes for now.
4. **Only QDOS work.** The trial runs on QDOS instructions only. Other providers' work stays in
   the normal mailboxes.
5. **Never copy in old mail.** The trial starts from a clean slate — don't bulk-forward
   historic emails into the mailbox, however tidy it seems. If an older email genuinely
   belongs to a live trial case, forward just that one.
6. **You don't need to add anything.** No covering note is needed; the system reads the
   provider's own document. If you do type something above the forward, that's fine — it's
   ignored for matching.

## What to expect

- The sender of a forward is you, not the provider — that's expected. The system identifies
  the work provider from the instruction document itself.
- A forwarded photo email finds its case by the registration or reference in the subject and
  photos. If it can't be matched it is held for manual attention rather than lost.
- If something doesn't appear as a case within a few minutes, tell the trial coordinator
  rather than re-forwarding it repeatedly — duplicates are handled safely, but the first copy
  is the one that matters.
