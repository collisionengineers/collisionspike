# Decisions and reviews

## Precedence

For an area covered by a manual review, the latest review is the authoritative requirement. A review may
correct an ADR, product page, architecture page, ticket, or implementation. Record the reconciliation in
the owning ticket and update the affected canonical source.

Outside reviewed areas, accepted ADRs govern hard-to-reverse choices. Proposed ADRs describe a candidate
decision only; they do not override accepted product requirements or observed live behaviour.

## Review handling

1. Inspect every image and read every review step.
2. Turn each actionable item into a tracked acceptance item.
3. Implement the change or record the explicit blocker.
4. Fill the review checklist honestly with the change and verification evidence.
5. Keep the original review material intact.

## Decision records

ADRs capture durable decisions and consequences, not implementation diaries. Exact resource state,
branch names, deployment hashes, ticket progress, and dated incident narration belong elsewhere.
