Collision Engineers · UX Design Lab · Round 2

Eight new UI directions — vet & pick one

A fresh batch, rebuilt to two corrections from round 1: no flow-explaining banners (efficiency-first — the app leads with the work, not an explanation of it) and grounded in how the real app actually works (a true five-tab case workspace, a three-kinds-of-number cockpit, and faceted queues — not a list box as the end state). Scores are advisory only. Nothing downstream runs until you pick.

Both corrections landed across all eight. Every direction ships a genuine five-tab case detail (12 EVA fields + provenance + evidence/photo-order + readiness + chasers + address/IBA), an un-conflated three-number cockpit, and faceted queues — and none carry onboarding/explainer banners. So this round differentiates on paradigm, taste, and efficiency, not compliance.
How to vet: click Cockpit first (the home — three kinds of number + the whole-inbox triage of Receiving work / Queries / Other + a verb-led chase-next worklist), then Queues and Case detail (the five-tab workspace). The order below is by advisory weighted score and is not a recommendation — the top four sit inside scoring noise. Pick on fit & taste.

Quiet Premium product-minimal91.6

Refined product-SaaS minimalism (Stripe/Linear-light): warm paper, one scarce teal, inline-edit-on-click everywhere, ⌘K + j/k, readiness ✗ that auto-opens the failing field. Efficiency wired, not drawn.

Suits: an operator who wants a quiet, premium, inline-edit-forward tool that ports to CE/Fluent with zero structural change.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: most product-true + portableRisk: "quiet" can read austere

Cold Slate Reader split-triage89.6

Superhuman-grade three-pane mail client: a persistent inbox list drives a reading pane and a detail pane, all on screen — "never leave the list" is genuinely wired (J/K · E · S · 1–5 · ⌘K). The round's efficiency benchmark + most domain-faithful build.

Suits: a power-user / high-volume triager who lives in the inbox and will learn keystrokes.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: efficiency benchmarkRisk: morphing centre pane is learned

Day Ledger agenda-ops88.0

A day-planner for the backlog: a banded oldest-due-first agenda makes priority spatial, so "what next?" needs no sort, legend, or second click — over a fully-wired dense five-tab workspace.

Suits: an operator who thinks of the backlog as a schedule to clear top-down, not a list to scan.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: kills the morning sortRisk: home is non-sortable by design

The Dossier case-file86.8

Tactile case-file: warm paper, manila section-tabs, a rubber-stamp status mark, a desk-tray inbox — borrowed spatial cognition that makes scanning muscle-memory. The most fully-built coverage in the round.

Suits: a team that values approachability + a distinctive, physically-familiar workspace and will buy the IA, not the texture.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: most built + most memorableRisk: skeuomorphism spent at port

Inbox-Zero focus-flow83.6

Single-task worklist: one spotlit "next" case beside a thin keyboard rail; submit-and-the-next-rises removes the choice of what to do next. Calm, warm, one big action.

Suits: an operator who wants the choice of what to do next removed and a calm, one-thing-at-a-time loop.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: efficiency poleRisk: deep but narrow (3 screens)

Bay Board pipeline-board83.2

Kanban pipeline: five calm colour-keyed lanes with one hot vermilion Review lane that turns "what's next" into a pre-attentive colour-snap; drag-to-advance cards. The board is the funnel you act on.

Suits: a team that wants to see and move the whole pipeline as one Kanban board.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: pipeline-at-a-glanceRisk: drag vs event-derived status

IDE Workbench workbench82.0

Daylight VS Code/Fleet: an explorer rail, multi-case editor tabs, and an always-open Problems/Readiness inspector whose every ✕ deep-links to the fix. Cleanest brand re-anchor of the round.

Suits: a technical power user who juggles several half-finished cases at once and likes IDE muscle-memory.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: parallel multi-case workRisk: IDE idioms must be learned

Data Grid grid-native79.6

Airtable/Notion-grade database workspace: dense inline-editable grids, frozen VRM + Case/PO columns, colour-coded select-chips, and a multi-select bulk-action bar to clear many partials in one pass (unique in the round).

Suits: an operator who triages in bulk and is comfortable with spreadsheet idioms.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: bulk-action throughputRisk: implies a configurable-DB product
