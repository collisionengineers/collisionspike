Eight UI directions — vet & pick one

Throwaway HTML explorations of the whole product. Open each direction's three core screens, compare, and choose the one to take forward. Scores are advisory only — decision-support, not a verdict. Nothing downstream runs until you pick.

How to vet: click Cockpit first (the main page — it manages the whole inbox: Receiving work / Queries / Other + new cases + queues snapshot), then Queues and Case detail. The order below is by advisory weighted score and is not a recommendation — the top three sit inside scoring noise. Pick on fit & taste.

Soft-Pack Bento bento-modular88

Warm putty sorting-tray; every region a flat white compartment in a debossed well with a colour-keyed protruding index tab. Friendly but disciplined.

Suits: an operator who wants work organised into legible trays + next-action obviousness.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: next-action obviousnessRisk: signature spent at port

Ruled Paper swiss-grid86.4

Strict Swiss grid, hairline rules, restrained palette; an indexed "Measure" gutter ties the 01–12 EVA contract into the system. Typography does the work.

Suits: precision-minded teams weighting brand-portability + structural rigour highest.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: cleanest CE re-skinRisk: runs cool / tall cockpit

Telemetry Deck dataviz-forward85

Dark instrument console; charts & metrics lead. A "Flow Channel" shunts stalled cases to a siding and renders dwell as a lengthening, reddening ruler.

Suits: ops-dashboard lovers who want a live console and will learn one signature device.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: top visual craftRisk: invented vocabulary to teach

Hearth-Bead soft-approachable83

Calm, warm, identity-forward; one tactile pipeline "bead" hero that doubles as dashboard, status model and nav. Lowest-stress feel of the set.

Suits: teams prioritising approachability + low-stress all-day use over authority/wow.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: most approachableRisk: largest brand-port distance

Graphite NOC command-center82.8

Near-black ops wallboard; liquid drain-gauge tiles, verb-led chase-next, full keyboard spine (j/k, Cmd-K, /). "What do I clear next" is one glance + one keystroke.

Suits: a power-user / high-volume operator who lives in the tool and learns keystrokes.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: best task efficiencyRisk: first-run learning cost

The Reading Room calm-editorial82.8

Magazine reading surface; editorial typography carries the discipline instead of charts, with a typographic "Contents Rule" pipeline. Lowest-fatigue, most distinctive.

Suits: operators who scan hundreds of times a day and value calm legibility over density.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: lowest-fatigue surfaceRisk: low density = more scrolling

Aurora Glass glass-depth77

Translucent blurred glass panels over a soft aurora ground; elevation = actionability, always labelled so it never leans on the effect alone. The most premium identity.

Suits: a demo/stakeholder-wow moment; teams who accept a plainer shipped result.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: most memorableRisk: identity spent at port

Concrete Ledger brutalist-utility76

Hard edges, heavy visible borders, borders-not-colour grammar; filled / outline / thermometer cells make the three-kinds-of-number rule pre-attentive. Honest by construction.

Suits: operators who want maximum honesty + speed and don't mind a raw, polarising look.

Cockpit Queues Case detail
Standout: pre-attentive number ruleRisk: on the coverage floor
